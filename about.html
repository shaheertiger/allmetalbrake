Our Story

Why We Built This Site

In 2019, one of our founders spent $1,400 on a sheet metal brake based on a glowing review from a website that clearly had never bent anything heavier than 22-gauge hobby tin. The brake crowned badly on 16-gauge, the bed wasn't square, and the manufacturer's support was nonexistent. It went back within two weeks.

That experience sparked All Metal Brake. We set a simple rule: we only publish scores after we've personally used a tool in a real production environment — minimum 90 days, minimum 500 bending cycles.

We are not journalists reviewing metalworking tools. We are metal workers who also happen to write. The difference matters.

What We Stand For

Our Editorial Values

No Free Samples

We purchase every tool at retail. Manufacturers cannot send us products for review — this eliminates the subtle pressure to be nice about a product you didn't pay for.

No Paid Rankings

Our rankings are determined entirely by test scores. An advertiser cannot buy a higher position. The #1 slot goes to the tool that earned it.

Transparent Methodology

Every review includes a full breakdown of our test protocol, the materials used, and the specific measurements taken. You can replicate our tests yourself.

We Update When Things Change

Manufacturers change QC between production runs. If a formerly excellent tool declines in quality, we update the score and say why. Old praise doesn't last forever here.
